Description

Abstract / Description:
This dataset shows the position, name and ownership of Railway centrelines within the State of Queensland.
Purpose:
To provide the position of Railway centrelines for use in topographic mapping and in the production of navigational and web based mapping applications.
Additional Information:
The data has been compiled from numerous sources to produce a state wide coverage displaying both public and private railways. Attributes within the data describe the source of the data and includes data obtained from:
- Queensland Rail
- Queensland Department of Transport and Main Roads
- Queensland Orthophotography
- Satellite Imagery
- Geoscience Australia

History:

Lineage statement:
A spatial and attribute upgrade of the data supplied by QRAIL in 2006 and Geoscience Australia (GA) was performed from July 2016 to June 2018 and is continuing to be performed on an ongoing basis. This current upgrade was completed in December 2019. The spatial upgrade used information from Queensland Rail railway line diagrams EAMS 04 Update 9, October 2019 and Aurizon line diagrams CNA Changes Review of June 2019 ARTC line diagram ARTCS3090006 of 2010. This has been combined with information from 1:100,000 Transverse Mercator topographic maps, heads-up digitising of digital aerial photography, information from the Queensland Digital Cadastral Database (DCDB) and data from privately owned railways. Updates have been made on all the data in Queensland except for a number of sugar cane railways. An attribute upgrade of rail gauges and the ownership of railway infrastructure has been performed over all of Queensland.

Some private railways such as cane lines are included when captured under the GA large scale mapping program or DNRME topographic capture programs. Geoscience Australia commenced this program of large scale revision mapping and data revision over Australia in partnership with state government mapping agencies and regional stakeholders. The data in these Work Packages have been captured in collaboration with the Queensland Department of Resources (DNRME). An attribute associated with every feature records the source of update information.
